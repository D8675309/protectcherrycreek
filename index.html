Case VA-2026-02 · Lake Laurie Drive, Valdosta

Protect Cherry Creek

A developer is requesting to rezone 5.2 acres of a 22-acre property next to our neighborhood from R-15 to R-M for eight 4-story buildings totaling 64 condominiums in Phase 1. The developer's own master plan envisions 121 total units across the full 22 acres. The Planning Commission votes March 30. City Council decides April 9.

The Evidence

See What They're Planning to Build

These are the developer's own documents filed with the City. The renderings show Phase 1 — 64 units in eight 4-story buildings. But the full master plan reveals 121 doors across 22 acres.

Developer Renderings Jefferson Architecture — "North Park" — 02/13/2026

Conceptual renderings showing the proposed 4-story buildings alongside single-family homes. This design is NOT legally binding — if rezoning is approved, the actual buildings can be taller, denser, and entirely different in appearance.

Official Engineering Site Plan Innovate Engineering & Surveying — Phase 1

Phase 1 site plan: 8 buildings, 8 units each = 64 units on 5.2 acres. Under R-M zoning, up to 113 units could be built on this parcel alone.

Property Location 2023 Aerial — VALOR GIS

The yellow outline shows the 5.2-acre parcel proposed for rezoning — directly adjacent to Cherry Creek homes and Lake Laurie.

Full 22-Acre Master Plan "Cove Concept Site Plan" — Cove Development (Connell / Trancygier / Greene)

The developer's own concept plan for the full 22-acre property. South half (top): 8 condo buildings with 64 units, lake park, and club house. North half (bottom): 51 single-family lots along Cherry Creek Road. Total: 121 doors. Phase 1 is just the foot in the door.

⚠
64 units is just Phase 1. The developer's own master plan shows 121 doors across 22 acres. Approving this vote sets the legal precedent for the full build-out. The concept plan is not binding — once R-M zoning is granted, there is no guaranteed limit on what gets built.
